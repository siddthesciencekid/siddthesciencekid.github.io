About

I am a junior at the University of Washington, currently pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science.

I am passionate about all things computer and my current interests include software development, web design and mobile applications. I also enjoy sharing my passion for computer science with others and it is one of the main reasons I was a TA for the intro web programming class at the University of Washington.

In addition to my regular curriculum, I like to participate in Hackathons and other related activities. I have also assisted in the development of a web site for a local non-profit cultural organization.

As a student I am seeking opportunities to develop my analytical, programming and managerial abilities as well as contributing to the development of innovative software.

In my free time I like to enjoy the outdoors and sing.

Neat: A solution for every home

Neat is a smart home concept that was researched and designed as part of a Human-Computer Interaction class at the University of Washington. Our group went into the field and conducted contextual inquries to understand problems people faced when trying to keep their home clean and applied design principles learned in class to present a solution to the problem. The end result was a design concept for a smart home that aimed to help busy users keep their house clean. As well as working on the project, I built the project website using bootstrap.

  • Project Website: Neat
  • Date: September-December 2015
  • Type: Design Concept

RoomEase

RoomEase is a roommate app that makes communicating with roommates and managing your household a lot easier. It provides four main features: writing shared lists among roommates, reserving household items or places at certain times, managing fridge content, and a reminders feed. I was primarly responsible for the front-end development of the app as well the JavaScript used to render some pages. Both the developer page and project page were also built by me.

  • Website: Developer Website --- Project Website
  • Date: January-March 2016
  • Type Mobile Application

UW Room Finder

UW Room Finder is a application currently under development to help students who are living in the University of Washington's dorm halls to be able to find open study rooms. The first phase of the project will tackle the ability of students to simply check the availibility of the multiple study rooms in the residence halls with later versions planning on including some sort of reservation system.
